Our Values

Student-Centred

We prioritise student needs and voices, enhancing their learning experience.

Collaboration

We work across teams to drive positive change in teaching and learning.

Inclusivity

We prioritise accessibility and inclusivity for all students.

Belonging

We create a supportive, welcoming environment where students feel empowered.

Development

We offer growth opportunities that prepare students for their careers.

Our Vision

We aim to create an inclusive environment where students actively contribute to shaping teaching and learning at the University of Manchester. Through innovation, collaboration, and a commitment to excellence, we strive to make a meaningful impact.

  1. Advertisement

    Positions for the Library Student Team will be advertised on CareerConnect for roughly 2-3 weeks at a time, we will also post alerts on our social media - make sure to keep your CareerConnect notifications on and follow our Instagram @UoMLibrary to keep an eye out for openings!


    The advert will include general information about the team, Our Work, and logistics such as pay rate and hours, as well as how to apply. It will also include key contact details for support, advice for completing application forms and preparing for group interviews, and information regarding our status as an equal opportunities employer.

  2. Application

    You will be asked to complete our application form before the advertised deadline. This will typically include answering 3-5 questions with 150 word or less answers aimed at evidencing your skills and experience relevant to the role. Whilst the questions are subject to change between each round, they will usually look at skills such as collaboration, time management, customer service, and digital capabilities. These applications are then compiled and reviewed against our listed requirements.


    In the event you are unsuccessful, all applicants will be notified with general guidance on improving your written application for next time but unfortunately, due to the volume of applications, we cannot provided personalised feedback at this stage.


    In the event you are successful, you will receive an invite to interview via email. This will include key information about how the interview will run, photos of the location, advice and suggested reading around our team and Our Work.

  3. Group Interview

    We like to think our interview stage is not your standard interview - we understand the term 'Group Interview' can be intimidating at first! Please refer to your invite to interview for specific information, as this is also subject to change between recruitment rounds.


    The room will be set up for group activities, facilitated by a member of the Library Team and at least two current Student Team Members. These activities are an opportunity for us to get to know you, you to get to know us, and to showcase your collaborative skills and your passions - there are no wrong answers. This is where you decide if we are the right fit for you, as much as we decide if you are the right fit for the team. Again, the activities may be subject to change between rounds, however they have historically included:

    1. A 1-to-1 Q&A-style activity for us to provide an overview of the role, discuss your own interests, and for you to ask questions and learn about current ST member's experiences.
    2. A Peer to Peer Support activity with an example enquiry we may get from a student to showcase your interpersonal and problem-solving skills.
    3. A Student Voice activity with an example scenario aimed at gathering student perspectives and representing different experiences, especially underrepresented groups.

    In the event you are unsuccessful, you will be notified with direct feedback - it is a competitive process, and we understand it can be stressful waiting around, so we aim to do this ASAP.


    In the event you are successful, congratulations! You will be notified with all relevant information and, if you choose to accept the offer, you are now a member of the Library Student Team!

  4. Induction

    Once you have accepted your offer, you will be invited to book your first shift which will be a general induction session. You will be introduced to the relevant Teaching, Learning and Students Staff, as well as the current TLS Interns and any ST members also on shift. You will receive our Handbook and Code of Conduct to explain expectations of the role, as well as familiarised with the digital systems we use for communications and organisation. We provide essential training such as fire safety, GDPR, and widening participation. We also recommend shadowing other ST members on their daily tasks, especially running our academic skills workshops both online and on-campus.

  5. Reflection and Feedback

    Student voice and experience are the backbone of the Library Student Team, so it is important to us that ST members have the opportunity to get involved with and develop the recruitment process for future applicants. You will be asked to provide your own reflection and feedback on how you found the recruitment process so we can continue to improve going forward, we encourage you to be as open and honest as possible. We always value student feedback and this is a value we carry throughout your entire time with us on the Library Student Team.
