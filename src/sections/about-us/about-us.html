General Introduction

Thank for you showing interest in joining the Library Student Team!
This page will give an overview of our approach to recruitment with a breakdown of each stage, including after you accept your offer to join the team.

STAGE 1: Advertisement

Positions for the Library Student Team will be advertised on CareerConnect for roughly 2-3 weeks at a time, we will also post alerts on our social media - make sure to keep your CareerConnect notifications on and follow our Instagram @UoMLibrary to keep an eye out for openings!
The advert will include general information about the team, what we do, and logistics such as pay rate and hours, as well as how to apply. It will also include key contact details for support, advice for completing application forms and preparing for group interviews, and information regarding our status as an equal opportunities employer.

STAGE 2: Application

You will be asked to complete our application form before the advertised deadline. This will typically include answering 3-5 questions with 150 word or less answers aimed at evidencing your skills and experience relevant to the role. Whilst the questions are subject to change between each round, they will usually look at skills such as collaboration, time management, customer service, and digital capabilities. These applications are then compiled and reviewed against our listed requirements.
In the event you are unsuccessful, all applicants will be notified with general guidance on improving your written application for next time but unfortunately, due to the volume of applications, we cannot provided personalised feedback at this stage.
In the event you are successful, you will receive an invite to interview via email. This will include key information about how the interview will run, photos of the location, advice and suggested reading around our team and what we do.

STAGE 3: Group Interview

We like to think our interview stage is not your standard interview - we understand the term â€˜Group Interview' can be intimidating at first! Please refer to your invite to interview for specific information, as this is also subject to change between recruitment rounds.
The room will be set up for group activities, facilitated by a member of the Library Team and at least two current Student Team Members. These activities are an opportunity for us to get to know you, you to get to know us, and to showcase your collaborative skills and your passions - there are no wrong answers. This is where you decide if we are the right fit for you, as much as we decide if you are the right fit for the team. Again, the activities may be subject to change between rounds, however they have historically included:
A 1-to-1 Q&A-style activity for us to provide an overview of the role, discuss your own interests, and for you to ask questions and learn about current ST member's experiences.
A Peer to Peer Support activity with an example enquiry we may get from a student to showcase your interpersonal and problem-solving skills.
A Student Voice activity with an example scenario aimed at gathering student perspectives and representing different experiences, especially underrepresented groups.
In the event you are unsuccessful, you will be notified with direct feedback - it is a competitive process, and we understand it can be stressful waiting around, so we aim to do this ASAP.
In the event you are successful, congratulations! You will be notified with all relevant information and, if you choose to accept the offer, you are now a member of the Library Student Team!

STAGE 4: Induction

Once you have accepted your offer, you will be invited to book your first shift which will be a general induction session. You will be introduced to the relevant Teaching, Learning and Students Staff, as well as the current TLS Interns and any ST members also on shift. You will receive our Handbook and Code of Conduct to explain expectations of the role, as well as familiarised with the digital systems we use for communications and organisation. We provide essential training such as fire safety, GDPR, and widening participation. We also recommend shadowing other ST members on their daily tasks, especially running our academic skills workshops both online and on-campus.

STAGE 5: Reflection and Feedback

Student voice and experience are the backbone of the Library Student Team, so it is important to us that ST members have the opportunity to get involved with and develop the recruitment process for future applicants. You will be asked to provide your own reflection and feedback on how you found the recruitment process so we can continue to improve going forward, we encourage you to be as open and honest as possible. We always value student feedback and this is a value we carry throughout your entire time with us on the Library Student Team.

FAQs

Q: What do the Library Student Team actually do?
A: We do lots of different things! As part of the Library staff, we help smoothly run the daily library service - including ensuring the academic skills workshops are student-oriented and beneficial for your studies. We answer student queries, centre the needs of all students, represent the voice of the student body at events on and off campus.
For further details of our key responsibilities, see the job description attached to the advertisement on CareerConnect.

Q: How often do you recruit?
A: We usually do 1-2 rounds of recruitment per academic year, however there are no set dates and are determined by the team's need and availability. Keep an eye out on CareerConnect and our social media for our next advertisement!

Q: What are your requirements?
A: Please see the relevant job description attached to the advertisement on CareerConnect, which lists our skills and qualification requirements in detail. We look for both Undergraduate* and Postgraduate students with strong interpersonal skills, effective communication, good attention to detail, organisational skills, and an overall enthusiastic approach to our projects.
That being said, we are always looking for new perspectives and pride ourselves on the diversity of lived experiences amongst members. There is no singular perfect student team member, all we ask for is your proactive participation in the continuing success of our team.
*We do ask that applicants are not currently in their final year of study

Q: Do you have any advice for future applicants?
A:Remember to be your authentic self and take pride in your current skillset, as well as the skills you want to develop. At every stage of the application process, we want to know about you and the things you are passionate about - always remember there is no wrong answer!
You can find advice for completing job application forms and preparing for group interviews via the Careers Service website.
Make sure to contact the team if you have any questions or require further support with the application process.

Q: Where can I find current ST members on campus?
A: We are based in the Main Library, and we can be identified by our bright red branded t-shirts. You will usually find us in the academic skills workshops, both in the Main Library and Alan Gilbert Learning Commons. We are also frequently found all around campus at different events, such as Open Days and Freshers Fairs. Feel free to have a chat with any of our friendly team members and we'll answer your questions as best we can!

Q: Can I specialise in a particular area or topic?
A: Once you are inducted onto the team, we do offer the opportunity to apply for one of our Area Lead roles. This allows you to specialise in a specific work area that interests you the most, taking the lead on project development. Each area will have 2 area leads, giving you someone to collaborate and bounce ideas off of, along with support from the wider TLS staff. Of course, you can also still get involved with all of the other projects going on, you are not restricted by this role at all. Areas change every now and then but frequently include topics such as academic material development, accessibility, access and success programmes, social media, library campaigns, environmental responsibility, data and feedback etc.

Q: Will this count towards my Stellify award?
A: Yes! This paid role will count towards the Step up and Lead requirement for Undergraduates and the Champions for Change requirement for Postgraduates. Look at the Stellify Award webpage and the FAQs for further details about how to achieve your Stellify Award before graduating.

Q: What opportunities and qualifications can this role offer me?
A: We prioritise the professional and personal development of our members during their time on the team. You will have plenty of opportunities to develop your leadership skills, network with professionals both in and out of the University, grow your CV, and achieve qualifications. You can find out more information about the team's achievements through our awards section.

Q: Are the working hours flexible?
A: Yes. We understand that you are busy students and encourage you to always prioritise your studies, so you book your own shifts according to your timetable. You will be expected to maintain a consistent level of commitment to the role, which will average 8 hours per week, with more flexibility around assessment period and term holidays.
